**RELEVANT (1 paper):**

**🟢 2603.03096 — "Interpreting Speaker Characteristics in the Dimensions of Self-Supervised Speech Features"**
(Van Rensburg, Kyle Janse; IEEE Signal Processing Letters, Interspeech region)
- **Method:** PCA on utterance-averaged WavLM representations
- **Key finding:** Principal dimension (most variance) = **pitch + gender**. Individual PCA dimensions correlate with intensity, noise level, second formant (F2), high-frequency characteristics.
- **Synthesis control:** Modifying individual PCA dimensions → controllable voice characteristics in synthesis
- **Direct connections:**
  1. **Gap #18 (Phonological Geometry through Connector):** Choi et al. 2602.18899 showed voicing/place/manner are linear in S3M space. This paper shows *speaker characteristics* are also decomposable in WavLM principal directions. Convergence: both confirm geometric organization of acoustic attributes in SSL features. Distinct: Choi = phonological features (voicing = consonant properties); Van Rensburg = speaker characteristics (pitch = prosodic, gender, formants). Together → complete picture of what's "naturally linear" in SSL space.
  2. **AudioSAEBench Category 0 (Audio-RAVEL):** RAVEL-style disentanglement requires knowing which attributes are truly separable. Van Rensburg confirms that speaker identity (gender), pitch, formant structure, and intensity are independently accessible via PCA → can design RAVEL interchange interventions at the dimension level. Also: phoneme-level (Choi) vs speaker-level (Van Rensburg) attributes are dissociable = good for Category 0 isolation experiments.
  3. **Paper B (AudioSAEBench) motivation:** If PCA dimensions already encode speaker characteristics, SAE features should too — but SAE should be *more* interpretable (monosemantic). Van Rensburg = baseline: "even without SAE, PCA finds speaker geometry." AudioSAE = step up: "SAE finds MORE interpretable, causally steerable features." Good narrative contrast for Paper B §1 or §2.2 motivation.
  4. **Audio T-SAE (Idea #7):** Van Rensburg uses utterance-AVERAGED representations (erases phoneme-level temporal variation). T-SAE would capture BOTH speaker-level (global) and phoneme-level (frame-local) structure — audio T-SAE fills the gap Van Rensburg leaves by time-averaging.
