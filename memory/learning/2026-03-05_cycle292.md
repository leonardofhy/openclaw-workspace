### ⭐⭐⭐ 2603.03855 — "A Sensitivity Analysis of Multi-Event Audio Grounding in Audio LLMs"
**Authors:** Taehan Lee et al. | Submitted to Interspeech 2026

**Problem:** Audio LLMs reliability in complex acoustic scenes (multi-event) unexplored at scale
**Method:**
- 71K AudioCapsV2 clips; extract normalized (source, attribute) events
- Two query types: present-event (ground truth detection) + absent-event (hallucination probe)
- Negative sampling via audio-aligned text embedding space (similarity-filtered)
- 4 SOTA Audio LLMs × 12 prompt variants × 500K yes/no queries per model

**Results:**
- As event count ↑: TPR ↓ + FPR ↑ (more hallucination under complexity)
- Prompts induce strong TPR/FPR trade-off (LLMs choose between precision and recall, not both)
- Models become MORE UNCERTAIN on multi-event audio (confidence → 0.5)

**Connection to T3 (Listen vs Guess):**
- BEHAVIORAL proof of the phenomenon gc(k) is designed to MECHANISTICALLY explain
- Paper A §1 cite: "behavioral analyses show degraded grounding under complexity [Lee et al. 2026] — we provide the mechanistic account via causal patching"
- The TPR/FPR trade-off = prompt-level manifestation of listen/guess switching = exactly what gc(k) measures at the layer level
- Gap: Lee et al. = BEHAVIORAL (black-box); Paper A = MECHANISTIC (circuit-level)
- Related: MPAR² connection (RL forces listening → reduces FPR under complexity)

**10 Core Questions:** Q6 ("when is model listening vs guessing?") — this paper answers behaviorally, Paper A mechanistically

### ⭐⭐ 2603.03359 — "ACES: Accent Subspaces for Coupling, Explanations, and Stress-Testing in ASR"
**Authors:** Swapnil Parekh et al. | (no venue yet)

**Problem:** ASR performance disparities across accents — internal mechanisms poorly understood
**Method:**
- ACES: representation-centric audit = extracts accent-discriminative subspaces via PCA/probing
- Model: Wav2Vec2-base | 5 English accents
- Key metrics: subspace dimensionality, WER correlation with projection magnitude, perturbation coupling strength

**Results:**
- Accent info concentrates in **low-dimensional early-layer subspace (layer 3, k=8)**
- Projection magnitude correlates with per-utterance WER (r=0.26)
- Subspace-constrained perturbations → stronger WER coupling (r=0.32) vs random-subspace (r=0.15)
- Linear attenuation of accent subspace DOES NOT reduce disparity — slightly worsens it
- Conclusion: accent features ENTANGLED with recognition-critical cues

**Connection to Papers A+B:**
- **Paper B (AudioSAEBench) — Gap #23 (Audio-RAVEL / Isolate metric) CONFIRMED:**

  - ACES cite in Paper B §4.1 Isolate metric motivation: "prior work shows accent features entangled with WER-critical cues [Parekh et al. 2026] — our Isolate metric quantifies this entanglement per feature"

### ⭐ 2603.03312 — "SemKey: Signal-Grounded Framework for EEG-to-Text Decoding"

**Problem:** EEG→text decoding prone to Signal Neglect (hallucination from linguistic priors)

- Evaluation: N-way Retrieval Accuracy + Fréchet Distance (not BLEU)
