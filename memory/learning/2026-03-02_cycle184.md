## Content: Asiaee 2026 — "Efficient Discovery of Approximate Causal Abstractions via Neural Mechanism Sparsification" (arXiv:2602.24266, Feb 27, 2026)

**Problem:** Finding causal abstractions (faithful high-level SCMs) from trained NNs normally requires brute-force interchange interventions or retraining — computationally prohibitive for large audio models.

**Core method:**
- Reframe causal abstraction search as **structured pruning** (searching over approximate abstractions)
- Treat trained NN as a deterministic SCM
- Derive **Interventional Risk objective** whose second-order expansion yields CLOSED-FORM criteria for:
  1. Replacing units with constants (prune/ablate)
  2. Folding units into neighbors (merge components)
- Under uniform curvature → score reduces to **activation variance** → recovers variance-based pruning as a special case
- Validates via interchange interventions (same evaluation as DAS/IIT)

**Key insight — "variance = approximate causal importance":**
- Activation variance is a first-order proxy for interventional risk
- This gives theoretical justification for why ablation studies using variance thresholds are approximately correct
- **When it fails:** Non-uniform curvature (complex loss landscapes) → variance overfits to frequent activations, misses rare-but-important causal components (= exactly the audio scenario where infrequent phoneme features may be causally critical)

**Connections to Paper A (Listen Layer):**
1. **Efficiency argument:** The Asiaee method could be used to pre-screen which layers are likely candidates for DAS intervention BEFORE running expensive IIT sweeps — rough activation variance map first, then targeted DAS at promising layers
2. **Theoretical framing:** Cite as "approximation justification" — why layer-level variance heatmaps (from whisper_hook_demo.py, cycle #12) already hint at the transition zone BEFORE running full DAS
3. **When it fails for audio:** Rare phoneme features with low activation variance but high causal weight → DAS is NECESSARY (not just variance-based pruning); Asiaee gap = Leo's argument for why DAS-IIT is the right method

**Connections to Sutter et al. (Gap #25) — theory triangle:**
- Sutter: non-linear maps → vacuous IIT → linearity is necessary for non-trivial causal abstraction
- Asiaee: efficient search via variance-based pruning → justified when curvature is uniform
- Geiger et al. (2301.04709): full DAS = correct, theoretically grounded
- **Triangle**: Asiaee + Geiger = efficient + correct; Sutter = guard against non-linear degeneration
- Paper A should cite all three: Geiger (foundation), Asiaee (efficiency), Sutter (linearity constraint)
