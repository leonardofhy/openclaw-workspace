## Content: Lin et al. 2502.17516 — "A Survey on Mechanistic Interpretability for Multi-Modal Foundation Models"

### Paper metadata
- **Authors**: Zihao Lin, Samyadeep Basu, Mohammad Beigi et al. (21 authors, UC Davis + UMD + Adobe + UIUC + Virginia Tech)
- **arXiv**: 2502.17516v1 (Feb 22, 2025) — 30 pages, 4 Figures, 10 Tables
- **Scope**: Survey of MI for multimodal foundation models (MMFMs)
- **Coverage**: Non-generative VLMs (CLIP), generative VLMs (LLaVA, Qwen), text-to-image diffusion models (Stable Diffusion)

### 3-Dimensional Taxonomy (the paper's organizing framework)
1. **Model Family**: contrastive VLMs, generative VLMs, text-to-image diffusion
2. **Interpretability Technique**: LLM-adapted methods vs. multimodal-specific methods
3. **Applications**: downstream uses of MI insights

### Methods Covered (from LLM MI, adapted to multimodal)
- **Linear Probing** (§4.1): Extended to multimodal; finding = *intermediate layers* capture cross-modal interactions (unlike LLMs where upper layers = semantic)
- **Logit Lens** (§4.2): Earlier layers more robust to misleading inputs in VLMs; hallucinated tokens have weaker attention in critical layers (Jiang et al.); confirms AudioLens insight
- **Causal Tracing / Circuit Analysis** (§4.3): Mid-layer MLPs crucial for factual recall in LLMs; adaptation to VLMs active area
- **Representation Decomposition** (§4.4): ICA/PCA/NMF on activations to disentangle components
- **Task Vectors** (§4.5): Task arithmetic in multimodal models
- **Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs)** (§4.6): "machine-discovered feature bases for granular manipulation"
- **Neuron-level descriptions** (§4.7): Concept-specific neurons in multimodal models

### Key Survey Findings (synthesis)
1. **LLM MI methods transfer with moderate adjustment** — treating visual and textual inputs similarly enables most methods to work
2. **Cross-modal interactions require new methods** — intermediate layers are the "action zone" in VLMs (not upper layers like in LLMs)
3. **Hallucination mitigation and model editing are underdeveloped** in multimodal models compared to LLMs
