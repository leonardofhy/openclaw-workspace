## Content: AG-REPA (Causal Layer Selection for Representation Alignment in Audio Flow Matching) — DEEP SCAN

**Paper**: arXiv:2603.01006 (Pengfei Zhang et al., Mar 2026, ICML submission)
**Domain**: Audio Flow Matching generation (TTS + TTA), NOT speech LLM understanding

### Core Finding — Store-Contribute Dissociation (SCD) — Spatiotemporally Resolved:

SCD is NOT uniform across layers — it has a spatiotemporal anatomy:
- **Deep layers (L20-24)** = Static Semantic Reservoirs → high LASP (representation score), low FoG-A (causal score); information-rich, functionally passive
- **Early layers (L1-3)** = Primary Causal Drivers → high FoG-A due to Jacobian sensitivity; low representation; drive velocity field
- **Middle layers (L6-12)** = Dynamic Transition Zone → functional importance shifts DURING denoising (peaks at t≈0.5); these are the layers that benefit most from AG-REPA alignment

Key quote: "alignment is most effective when applied to the causally dominant layers that drive the velocity field, rather than to layers that are representationally rich but functionally passive"

### NEW CITES for Paper A (from AG-REPA §2.3):

1. **Klabunde et al. 2025** — survey of representational similarity metrics; cautions that "high similarity does not imply functional equivalence" → cite in Paper A §1 as general principle

3. **Hase et al. 2023** — "layers storing factual knowledge are not necessarily the most effective targets for model editing" → existing support in text LM editing literature, convergent with Paper A claim; AG-REPA cites this as precedent

### NUANCE for Paper A: SCD Direction Differs by Task

**Critical point**: In audio GENERATION (AG-REPA), early layers (L1-3) are causally dominant. But in speech UNDERSTANDING (Whisper encoder), the Triple Convergence transition zone is at ~50% depth (layer 6-7 of 12 = middle). These are DIFFERENT systems with DIFFERENT functional profiles:
- Generation: velocity field = causal target; early layers drive trajectory
- Understanding: representation decision = causal target; middle layers = acoustic→semantic transition

**Paper A implication**: Don't over-claim SCD as universal. Frame it as: "SCD is a general phenomenon (generation domain: AG-REPA; editing domain: Hase et al.; general theory: Braun et al.); we show it manifests specifically in speech UNDERSTANDING at the middle-layer transition zone."

### Gap Connection:
- SCD in generation (early-dominant) + Triple Convergence in understanding (middle-dominant) → same meta-principle ("stores ≠ causes"), different implementations. Paper A contributes the speech UNDERSTANDING instance.
