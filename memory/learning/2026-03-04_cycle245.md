### 2. AG-REPA (arXiv:2603.01006) — Scan + Key Insights

**"Attribution-Guided REPresentation Alignment for Audio Flow Matching"**
Zhang et al., submitted Mon Mar 1, 2026

**Key concepts:**
- **Store-Contribute Dissociation (SCD)**: layers that best *store* semantic/acoustic information (high teacher-space cosine similarity) ≠ layers that *contribute* most causally to the velocity field that drives generation
- **FoG-A** (Forward-only Gate Ablation): quantifies each layer's causal contribution by the induced change in predicted velocity field — sparse layer selection + adaptive weighting
- Results: AG-REPA consistently outperforms REPA baseline (unified LibriSpeech + AudioSet training)
- "Alignment is most effective when applied to causally dominant layers rather than representationally rich but functionally passive ones"

**Connection to Leo's work:**
- **SCD = direct empirical support for Paper A's core hypothesis!** The "Triple Convergence Hypothesis" predicts a specific layer L* where representation richness AND causal contribution peak together — or more precisely, where audio information is causally "used" by the LM, not just "stored." AG-REPA shows these can dissociate → makes Leo's paper MORE interesting (not less). The "Listen Layer" is specifically the layer where audio is CAUSALLY CONSULTED, not just represented.
- **Paper A §2 Related Work**: Add AG-REPA. "Recent work in audio generative models independently discovers layer-level store-contribute dissociation (SCD, Zhang et al. 2026); we provide the first causal characterization of this phenomenon in speech LLMs using DAS-grounded gc(L)."
- **Terminology opportunity**: gc(L) = "contribution coefficient" > "grounding coefficient" may be more precise — audio grounding = whether the layer causally CONTRIBUTES, not just stores. Consider renaming for clarity in Paper A.
- **No competitor threat**: AG-REPA = audio generation (flow matching), not speech LLM understanding/grounding. Different model family, different research question. 🟢 Gap still open.

### 3. Maghsoudi & Mishra (2602.01247) — Full Deep-Read

**"Mechanistic Interpretability of Brain-to-Speech Models Across Speech Modes"**
Submitted Feb 1, 2026. Ayushi Mishra (likely PhD student)

**Problem**: Brain-to-speech decoding works across vocalized/mimed/imagined speech, but WHY? What are the internal mechanisms?

**Methods used** (ALL highly relevant to Paper A methodology):
- **Cross-mode activation patching** — patch activations from vocalized→imagined to test cross-mode transfer (same as Leo's audio→text context patching)
- **Tri-modal interpolation** — smooth blending test: are speech modes discrete or continuous? (Result: continuous causal manifold — speech modes don't jump discretely)
- **Coarse-to-fine causal tracing** — layer-level → head-level → neuron-level localization cascade
- **Causal scrubbing** (Mathwin & Chan, 2022) — verify if causal structure is sufficient
- **Neuron-level activation patching** — finds "small but NOT distributed subsets" of neurons mediate cross-mode transfer

**Key findings:**
1. Speech modes lie on a **shared continuous causal manifold** (not discrete)
2. Cross-mode transfer = mediated by **compact, layer-specific subspaces** (not diffuse activity)
3. **Hierarchical + direction-dependent** representational structure across speech modes
4. Small subsets of neurons (not individual units, not fully distributed) = the "sweet spot" of localization

**Implications for Paper A:**
- Validates that "compact causal subspaces" are a real finding, not an artifact — exactly what DAS should find for audio grounding in speech LLMs
- **"Shared continuous manifold"** = the geometry connecting audio tokens to text-prediction space has gradients, not sharp boundaries → supports the DAS rotation approach (vs. step-function patching)
- Coarse-to-fine cascade (layer → head → neuron) = a methodological template for Paper A: gc(L) curve → head-level gc → feature-level gc (Paper B)
- **Related work placement**: Maghsoudi is the closest existing MI work on speech models using activation patching. Paper A must cite this and differentiate: "We study SPEECH LLMs (S3M encoder + LM), not brain-to-speech decoders; our gc(L) quantifies audio-vs-text causal dominance, not cross-mode transfer."
- **Methodological note**: They use causal scrubbing (Mathwin & Chan 2022) as a verification step — worth considering as a secondary validation for Paper A's Listen Layer claim.

**New Gap formalized from Maghsoudi:**
- Gap #26: Maghsoudi finds compact causal subspaces in brain-to-speech (custom model). **No equivalent study exists for pre-trained, large-scale speech LLMs (Whisper/HuBERT encoder → Qwen2-Audio LM)**. Leo's Paper A is exactly this.
- Connection to triple convergence: if speech modes share a continuous causal manifold in B2S models, the audio-text grounding likely also forms a continuous gradient (not a binary "listen/don't-listen" step) → gc(L) curve should be smooth, with a peak rather than a step. Testable prediction: Paper A Figure 2 should show a smooth gc(L) curve.
