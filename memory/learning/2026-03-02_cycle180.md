### What changed in paper-b-pitch.md (v0.7 → v0.8)

**1. New Category 0: Audio-RAVEL — Causal Disentanglement**
- Derived from RAVEL (Huang et al., ACL 2024, Gap #23 identified in cycle #179)
- Two-score metric: Cause(F, A) + Isolate(F, A) → RAVEL-audio = harmonic mean
- Audio hypothesis: audio SAEs will exhibit MORE cross-attribute leakage than text SAEs due to acoustic co-occurrence (voicing ↔ gender, noise ↔ emotion in training corpora)
- Stimulus design: Choi et al. 2602.18899 minimal phonological pairs (voicing/manner/place) + TTS-augmented pairs
- Ceiling baseline: MDAS (multi-task DAS from RAVEL) — simultaneously optimizes all attribute subspaces orthogonal
- Gap filled: all 5 audio SAE papers measure Cause (steering success) but ZERO measure Isolate. Audio-RAVEL is the first to do both.
- Connection to Gap #22 (cycle #177): AudioSAE's "50% consistent features" claim is incomplete — consistency ≠ causality. A consistent-but-epiphenomenal feature scores high Cause but fails Isolate. Audio-RAVEL exposes this distinction.

**2. Category 4 upgrade: Three-metric Causal Controllability protocol**
Derived from Heimersheim & Nanda (2024) read in cycle #178:
- `ablation_d`: AND-gate test (necessity) — ablating feature degrades performance
- `steering_precision`: OR-gate test (sufficiency) — activating feature produces behavior
- `hydra_compensation`: quantifies Hydra effect (backup pathway compensation; ~0.7x per H&N) — top-K aggregate required for reliable attribution
- Audio denoising preferred over noising (OR-gate dominant in audio; noising creates OOD activations)
