**1. arXiv:2504.04479 — "Activation Patching for Interpretable Steering in Music Generation"**
(Facchiano, Strano, Crisostomi et al., Apr 2025, cs.SD — Rome/Sapienza group)
- **Method:** Difference-in-means steering vectors computed on curated prompt pairs (fast/slow tempo, bright/dark timbre) → injected into intermediate activations of a large text-to-music model
- **Key findings:** Direction vectors in audio model residual streams control musical attributes; identified layers with greatest influence; fine-grained control while preserving audio quality
- **Claims:** "First study of latent direction vectors in large audio models" (Apr 2025)

**2. arXiv:2602.01247 — "Mechanistic Interpretability of Brain-to-Speech Models Across Speech Modes"**
(Maghsoudi & Mishra, Feb 1 2026, cs.LG)
- **Method:** cross-mode activation patching + causal tracing + causal scrubbing + neuron-level patching; brain-to-speech decoder trained on vocalized/mimed/imagined speech
- **Key findings:**
  - Speech modes lie on a **shared continuous causal manifold** (not discrete states)
  - Cross-mode transfer mediated by **compact, layer-specific subspaces** (not diffuse activity)
  - Small but not isolated subsets of neurons (subspaces, not single units) mediate transfer
  - Hierarchical + direction-dependent representational structure
- **Relevance to goals:**
  - **Strong methodological alignment with Paper A**: uses the SAME toolset (activation patching + causal tracing + causal scrubbing + neuron-level analysis)
  - "Compact layer-specific subspaces" = directional confirmation for Listen Layer Hypothesis (Track 3) — information is localized at specific layers
  - Uses causal scrubbing (Goldowsky-Dill et al.) which Paper A §2 should cite for completeness
  - DIFFERENT domain (brain-to-speech ≠ speech LLM) → not a competitor; useful methodological parallel
  - **Previously flagged in cycle #3 field scan but NOT deeply read**
- **Gap connection:** None new — but CONFIRMS that audio patching + causal tracing = viable methodology; validates Paper A's approach
