### New Paper: Facchiano et al. 2504.04479 — "Activation Patching for Interpretable Steering in Music Generation"
**Group**: Sapienza Univ. Rome (Rodolà lab)
**Submitted**: April 6, 2025 (cs.SD)

**Core method:**
- Applies **difference-in-means steering vectors** to MusicGen (large audio model)
- Binary concepts: tempo (fast vs. slow), timbre (bright vs. dark)
- Inject scaled steering vectors into intermediate activations → fine-grained musical attribute modulation
- Compares injection layers → identifies layers with greatest influence
- Does NOT do activation patching in the IIT/causal tracing sense — this is steering (additive direction injection), not interventional patching (substitution from clean→corrupt)

**Key findings:**
- Direction vectors exist in MusicGen residual stream for musical attributes
- Steering coefficient controls attribute intensity continuously
- Layer injection strategy matters (mid-layers > early/late)

**Relevance to Leo's research:**
- **NOT a competitor to Paper A** — focus is music *generation* (decoder), not speech *understanding* (encoder). No causal tracing, no audio-vs-text pathway test.
- **Adjacent to AudioSAE Paper B**: validates that direction-based steering works in audio domain (consistent with AudioSAE steering results). Adds another reference for Track 2.
- **Closer to "Steering" than "Patching"** per Heimersheim & Nanda terminology: they use difference-in-means (semantic steering), NOT denoising patch from corrupted input. Correctly classified as SKIP for Paper A threat assessment.
- **NOT in existing literature review** — add as "related but different" for Paper B (completeness).

**Gap assessment:**
