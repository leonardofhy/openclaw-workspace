## Paper: AudioSAE (arXiv 2602.05027, EACL 2026)
"Towards Understanding of Audio-Processing Models with Sparse AutoEncoders"
Aparin et al. — trains SAEs across ALL encoder layers of Whisper + HuBERT.

## Key Findings

**Problem:** SAEs for audio underexplored; how do sparse features distribute across layers in speech encoders?

**Method:** Train SAEs on every Whisper/HuBERT encoder layer. Measure stability (cross-seed), interpretability, disentanglement, steering utility.

**Results:**
1. **>50% features stable across random seeds** — directly connects to Q082 Schelling stability
2. Reconstruction quality preserved across layers
3. Features capture acoustic (phonemes, noise) AND semantic (events, paralinguistic: laughter/whispering)
4. Disentanglement: removing only **19–27% of features** erases a concept → high feature specificity
5. **Feature steering → 70% reduction in Whisper false speech detections**, negligible WER impact → T5 safety probe relevance!
6. SAE features correlate with **human EEG during speech perception** → bridges audio-MI with cognitive neuroscience
