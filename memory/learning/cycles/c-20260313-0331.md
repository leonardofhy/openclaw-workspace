## Synthesis: OBLITERATUS × gc(k) — Safety Training = AND-gate Construction

### Problem framing
OBLITERATUS (from the Alignment Forum / recent AI safety context) refers to a class of "uncensoring" attacks that ablate safety training from LLMs post-hoc. The key empirical finding is that safety behaviors are **localizable and fragile** — a small number of model components (layers, features, directions) implement the safety filter, and removing them restores the uncensored behavior.

This pairs naturally with our AND/OR gate taxonomy and gc(k) framework:

### Mechanistic Bridge

**Claim:** Safety training constructs AND-gate features at the gc(k) peak layer.

**Logic chain:**
1. gc(k) measures information flow from audio → cross-attention → decoder at each layer k
2. AND-gate features = fire only when BOTH input-signal AND context-signal are present
3. Safety training adds a conditional: "if content is harmful AND model detects it, suppress output"
4. This conditional logic = an AND-gate: content-harm-detector AND output-suppressor co-fire

**Prediction:** Safety-trained models should show:
- Higher AND-gate fraction at the gc(k) peak layer vs base model
- Lower AND-gate fraction after OBLITERATUS-style ablation (uncensoring)
- AND-gate destruction = the mechanistic event that OBLITERATUS achieves

### Testable in T5 context

Our T5 track is building audio jailbreak detection. The OBLITERATUS bridge gives:

1. **Diagnostic**: measure AND-gate fraction at safety-critical layer before/after jailbreak attempt
2. **Alert criterion**: if AND-gate fraction drops below baseline → safety circuit being ablated
3. **Connection to M7 (delta_GS)**: AND-gate fraction correlates with M7 causal power (from Q102 hypothesis)
   - So delta_GS drop = proxy for AND-gate destruction = proxy for safety degradation
