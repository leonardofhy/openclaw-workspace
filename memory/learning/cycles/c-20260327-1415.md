## 400-word Prose

### How AND-frac Advances the State of the Art

Existing mechanistic interpretability methods fall into two broad families: **correlation-based feature extractors** (probing, CAV, SAE, attention rollout, logit lens) and **causal localization methods** (ROME/MEMIT). AND-frac bridges both families while adding a third capability: **commitment detection in streaming decoders**.

**Linear probing** trains a classifier on intermediate representations to test whether a concept is linearly encoded. While powerful for static analysis, probing is inherently correlational — a high probe accuracy says nothing about whether that representation *causes* downstream behavior. AND-frac's causal patching experiments (Q001–Q002) directly demonstrate intervention effects on WER, something probing cannot provide.

**TCAV / CAV** extends probing by defining human-interpretable concept vectors and measuring their alignment with activation derivatives. This is valuable for vision models where concepts (e.g., "stripes") are easy to define, but audio/speech concepts are far harder to operationalize. AND-frac requires no human-defined concepts — it emerges from the entropy of attention head distributions.

**Sparse Autoencoders (SAE)** decompose MLP activations into sparse, potentially monosemantic features. SAE is a promising direction for fine-grained feature discovery, but: (a) it targets MLP features, not attention dynamics; (b) audio SAEs are untested at scale; (c) SAEs provide no native commitment-point detection. AND-frac is complementary to SAEs (AND-frac for commitment detection; SAE for feature decomposition).

**Attention rollout** propagates attention weights backward to estimate information flow from input tokens. It captures *what* the model attends to, but treats all attention heads symmetrically — blending "listen" and "guess" heads that have qualitatively different computational roles. AND-frac explicitly separates these two modes, revealing when the model transitions from data-driven (AND, attend-to-input) to prior-driven (OR, guess-from-prior) processing.

**Logit lens** projects intermediate residual states onto the vocabulary to track how predictions form across layers. It is read-only and provides no mechanism for intervention. AND-frac's L* (commitment layer) can be interpreted as a logit-lens milestone — the layer at which the AND→OR transition stabilizes the prediction — but with the additional power of causal intervention.

**ROME / MEMIT** are causal editing methods for factual knowledge in language models. They are designed for text, depend on factual recall structure, and have no natural extension to audio. AND-frac is the first causal interpretability method designed natively for streaming speech decoders.

**Summary**: AND-frac is novel because it is (1) causal, (2) unsupervised, (3) audio-native, (4) captures commitment dynamics, and (5) lightweight enough for real-time monitoring.
