## Paper: Emotion-Sensitive Neurons in LALMs

**Ref:** arXiv:2601.03115 (Zhao, Schuller, Sisman — Jan 2026, 16p)
**Title:** Discovering and Causally Validating Emotion-Sensitive Neurons in Large Audio-Language Models

### Problem
LALMs achieve strong emotion recognition but we lack mechanistic account of *how* emotion is encoded internally.

### Method
- Neuron-level interpretability study on Qwen2.5-Omni, Kimi-Audio, Audio Flamingo 3
- Compare 4 neuron selectors: frequency, entropy, magnitude, contrast
- Inference-time interventions: **ablation** (degrades target emotion) + **gain amplification** (steers to target)
- Test on multiple emotion recognition benchmarks

### Results
- Ablating ESNs selected for emotion X → disproportionately degrades class X, preserves others ✅ causal
- Gain amplification → steers predictions toward target emotion ✅ controllable
- ESNs show **non-uniform layer-wise clustering** with partial cross-dataset transfer
- Effects work with modest identification data; scale with intervention strength
