## Paper
**Maghsoudi & Mishra (Feb 2026). "Mechanistic Interpretability of Brain-to-Speech Models Across Speech Modes."** arXiv:2602.01247

---

## Problem
Brain-to-speech (B2S) decoders work across vocalized, mimed, and imagined speech — but nobody understands the **internal mechanisms** behind this. Are the performance differences between speech modes caused by input signal properties, or by internal representation failures? Prior work = correlational probes, architectural ablations. No causal account.

## Method
Domain: sEEG neural recordings (VocalMind dataset: vocalized / mimed / imagined speech, single participant).
Architecture: Conv1D encoder → bidirectional GRU (3-layer, 256 hidden) → mel-spectrogram prediction → neural vocoder.
MI tools applied:
1. **Cross-mode activation patching** — swap conv_out or rnn_out between speech modes, measure decoding quality change
2. **Tri-modal interpolation** — linearly interpolate activations between modes to test discrete vs continuous representation
3. **Coarse-to-fine causal tracing** — layer-level → head-level → neuron-level localization
4. **Causal scrubbing** — test which subspaces are sufficient for cross-mode transfer
5. **Neuron-level activation patching** — granular necessity analysis

## Key Results
1. **Continuous shared manifold**: Speech modes lie on a shared continuous causal manifold (NOT discrete; interpolation works smoothly). This is a geometry finding.
2. **Direction asymmetry**: Patching vocalized→imagined RECOVERS decoding quality. Patching imagined→vocalized **catastrophically degrades** it. = vocalized > imagined in representation quality/richness; not symmetric.
3. **Compact subspaces**: Cross-mode transfer is mediated by **compact, layer-specific subspaces** (not diffuse network-wide activity). Conv = spatial features; GRU = temporal dynamics.
4. **Small neuron subsets**: Not isolated neurons, but **small non-distributed subsets** of neurons carry cross-mode transfer information. Neuron-level patching reveals this.
5. **Hierarchical structure**: Direction-dependent representational hierarchy; model captures causal hierarchy across modes.

## Critical Assessment
**Strengths**: First MI paper applied to B2S; clean experimental design; multiple converging tools (causal tracing + scrubbing + neuron patching); continuous manifold finding is novel.

**Weaknesses / Gaps Leo should note**:
1. **Single participant**: VocalMind = one sEEG subject → generalizability unknown
2. **Small model**: Conv1D + 3-layer GRU = not a transformer. MI findings may not transfer to transformer-based B2S or audio-LLMs directly.
3. **No SAE analysis**: They find small neuron subsets but don't decompose into monosemantic features → SAE follow-up = natural extension (Gap #32 candidate)
4. **No quantitative subspace size**: "compact" is qualitative — how many dimensions? DAS would give this number.

**Relevance to Leo**: HIGH for Paper A §2 (related work, parallel methods). LOW as direct methodological template (different architecture). **Cite in Paper A §2 as: "MI tools find compact subspaces in speech decoding [Maghsoudi 2026]; we test analogous structure in audio-LLMs."**
