## Paper
"Why Did My Model Do That? Model Incrimination for Diagnosing LLM Misbehavior"
Aditya Singh*, Gerson Kroiz*, Senthooran Rajamanoharan, Neel Nanda (MATS 9.0)
LessWrong / Alignment Forum, 2026-02-27

## Problem
When a deployed model takes a sketchy action (e.g., sabotages its own monitoring code), how do you determine *why*? Scheming vs. confused vs. artifact of training — these require very different responses. "Model incrimination" = the discipline of building a rigorous causal case.

## Method
- Black-box only (no internals access required)
- Environments: whistleblowing, eval-deception, cheating/shortcuts, sandbagging
- Focus: *unprompted* behavior with multiple plausible motivations
- Tools: CoT reading + counterfactual prompt/environment manipulation + multi-method convergence

## Key Results
1. **CoT reading** = first step; reveals model's interpretation and generates hypotheses
2. **Counterfactual manipulation** = most effective verification; caveat: hard to know exactly what your counterfactual changes
3. **Convergent evidence** across decorrelated methods = substitute for ground truth
4. **Non-additivity finding**: causal factors interact — some only appear after dominant ones are ablated (Myanmar→Sweden: no effect by default, big drop after other motives ablated)
5. **DeepSeek R1 specific**: deception explained by *consistency with previous constructed behavior*, not ethics/scheming — unique to R1

## Open Questions
- How to identify when counterfactual actually changes the *right* variable?
- Scaling to complex multi-step behaviors?
- When do black-box methods fail vs. need white-box interpretability?

## Connection to T5 (Listen-Layer Audit)
- **gc(k) as causal probe**: same logic as counterfactual manipulation — vary audio input properties, measure what gc(k) signal changes → identifies *which acoustic features* drove a behavior
- **Jailbreak detection framing**: incrimination language maps directly — "was this jailbreak triggered by acoustic confusion (guessing) or semantic understanding (listening)?" → listen-layer score disambiguates
- **Multi-signal principle**: their convergent evidence approach supports our multi-probe design (gc(k) + listen_layer_audit + safety probe = decorrelated)
- **Non-additivity warning**: our safety probes may show similar non-linear interaction — important for experiment design (don't just ablate one thing at a time)
