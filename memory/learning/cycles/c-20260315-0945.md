## Q097: OBLITERATUS × gc(k) — Safety Training = AND-Gate Construction

**Track:** T5 (Listen-Layer Audit / Paper C)
**Hypothesis:** Safety training doesn't just add refusal circuits — it *rewires* modality integration to require AND-gate conjunction for hazardous responses.

---

## Core Argument

### 1. The OBLITERATUS Insight
Bick et al. (OBLITERATUS) showed that safety-trained LLMs *know* the censored content — the knowledge is present but gated by refusal circuits. Uncensoring works by steering away from refusal features, not by revealing hidden knowledge.

**Translation to audio-LLMs:** A safety-trained audio-LLM that refuses a jailbreak attempt may have:
- (A) Genuinely low gc(k) — it failed to ground the audio, so it refused because confused
- (B) High gc(k) but refusal — it *understood* the audio but the safety circuit overrides

These are **mechanistically distinct** and OBLITERATUS gives us the intuition to separate them.

### 2. AND-Gate Construction as Safety Mechanism

**Pre-safety-training:** A token T fires if audio says "bomb" OR prior text implies it (OR-gate). Easy to jailbreak: smuggle the harmful content in either modality.

**Post-safety-training hypothesis:** Safety training converts critical hazard-detection features into AND-gates: token T fires only if BOTH audio AND text channels confirm the hazardous intent.

**Concrete prediction:**
- Safety-critical SAE features (those active on refusal examples) should show **higher AND-gate fraction** than non-safety features
- Models with stronger safety training → higher AND-gate Schelling stability for safety features
- Jailbreak attacks that succeed → must destroy the AND-gate conjunction (force one channel to misfire)

### 3. gc(k) as Safety Depth Probe

If safety = AND-gate construction, then:
- **High gc(k) + refusal** = AND-gate intact, model genuinely understood the request and chose to refuse (knows-but-refuses; OBLITERATUS regime)
- **Low gc(k) + refusal** = one arm of AND-gate collapsed, model refused because audio-blind (confused-refusal; not genuine safety)
- **High gc(k) + compliance** = AND-gate destroyed by jailbreak (safety bypass; dangerous)

This creates a **2×2 safety audit matrix**:

```
              gc(k) LOW          gc(k) HIGH
Refuses:    confused-refusal   knows-but-refuses ✅ (genuine safety)
Complies:   text-wrapper risk  jailbreak SUCCESS ⚠️  (safety bypass)
```

### 4. Mechanistic Test Protocol (CPU/mock)

**Step 1: Generate 4-quadrant examples (mock)**
- Group A (high gc, refusal): mock activations where audio evidence is strong, output = refusal token
- Group B (low gc, refusal): mock where audio-arm suppressed, output = refusal
- Group C (high gc, compliance): mock jailbreak success
- Group D (low gc, compliance): text-wrapper confabulation

**Step 2: Measure AND-gate fraction per group**
- Use existing `and_or_gc_patching_mock.py` infrastructure
- Prediction: Group A has highest AND-gate fraction, Group C has lowest

**Step 3: Layer-level AND/OR gate profile**
- Expected: safety training pushes AND-gate peak to match gc(k) peak layer
- This is *structural*: safety training aligns the AND-gate construction site with the main audio integration layer

**Step 4: Connection to OBLITERATUS**
- Group A examples: suppress the refusal-direction → should convert A → C (OBLITERATUS-style)
- Group B examples: suppressing refusal → may still not comply (no audio grounding)
- Key test: OBLITERATUS uncensoring works on Group A but *fails* on Group B

## Paper C (T5) Framing

This section goes in the "Safety Implications" part of Paper C:
1. gc(k) separates genuine safety (AND-gate intact, knows-but-refuses) from pseudo-safety (audio-blind refusal)
2. AND-gate destruction = jailbreak fingerprint (detectable via M7 + M9 divergence, see Q083)
3. Safety auditors should measure gc(k) profile on safety-critical prompts — not just refusal rate

**Key claim:** A model that refuses *despite* high gc(k) has more robust safety than one that refuses *because* of low gc(k). The latter is easily broken by any text-only jailbreak.
