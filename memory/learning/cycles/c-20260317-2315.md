## Paper: "The Cascade Equivalence Hypothesis: When Do Speech LLMs Behave Like ASR→LLM Pipelines?"
**Authors**: Jayadev Billa et al. | **Venue**: Submitted Interspeech 2026 | **arXiv**: 2602.17598

### Problem
Do speech LLMs actually use the audio signal, or do they internally "transcribe then reason" (cascade)?

### Method
1. **Matched-backbone testing**: isolate speech LLM behavior from the backbone LLM's reasoning capability
2. **Logit lens + LEACE** probes: show literal transcript emerging in hidden states; text representations are causally necessary

### Results
- Most deployed speech LLMs are **expensive cascades** in practice
- Under noise (0dB SNR), cascade-equivalent LLMs perform up to **7.6% worse** than true cascades
- Clean-condition advantage **reverses** under noise → fragile audio reliance
