### Problem
SAE features vary substantially across random seeds and training choices — instability undermines mechanistic interpretability.

### Method
- Add L1 or L2 penalties on **encoder and decoder weights** (not just activation sparsity L1)
- Evaluate interaction with common SAE training defaults (e.g., top-k, unit norm decoder)

### Key Distinction for Paper B (Q076/Q088)
| Type | Mechanism | Our Work |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Weight-reg stability | Training objective (L1/L2 on weights) | ❌ Not this |
| Schelling stability (M10) | **Evaluation metric** — cross-seed feature agreement rate | ✅ Our target |

Schelling stability measures: "do independently trained SAEs recover the same features?"  
Weight regularization may *increase* Schelling scores as a side effect, but is not the same concept.
