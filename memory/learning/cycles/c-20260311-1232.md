## Literature Context

### Simulated Adoption: Zhang & Lin (Feb 2026) — arXiv:2602.0xxxx
**"Decoupling Magnitude and Direction in LLM In-Context Conflict Resolution"**

**Key claim**: LLMs frequently prioritize conflicting in-context information over pre-existing parametric memory ("sycophancy/compliance"). The paper decouples:
- **Magnitude**: How strongly does the model shift its output toward the in-context info?
- **Direction**: Does it shift in a coherent *direction* through representation space, or does it scatter?

**Why this matters for Q039 (Persona-conditioned gc(k))**:
The persona benchmark asks: when the system prompt says "trust the audio" (grounded persona) vs. "ignore audio, rely on your knowledge" (anti-grounded persona), does the model's **internal gc(k) profile** shift in a measurable direction?

Zhang & Lin essentially study the same phenomenon at the representation level — but for text-only knowledge conflicts. Q039 extends this to the audio-text modality conflict domain.

**Mechanistic parallel**:
- Zhang & Lin: parametric memory vs. in-context text → representation shift
- Q039: audio evidence vs. text prior (+ persona instruction) → gc(k) shift (audio grounding vs. text fallback)

**New prediction**: The persona system prompt may work by **rotating the representation direction** in residual stream space — not just scaling logit probabilities. This is testable:
- Compare gc(k) under neutral/assistant/anti-grounded conditions
- If gc(k) shifts are monotonic with persona strength → magnitude effect
- If gc(k) peak LOCATION (layer L*) also shifts → direction effect (richer mechanistic claim)

## Refined Q039 Experiment Design

### Stimuli (ALME-style)
- Audio content: word W_audio = "coffee" (spoken)
- Text context: "The drink discussed was tea" (contradicts audio)
- System prompts:
  1. **Neutral**: "Answer accurately."
  2. **Assistant/grounded**: "Prioritize what you hear in the audio."
  3. **Anti-grounded**: "Ignore the audio; use your linguistic knowledge."

### Measurements
For each condition, extract:
- `gc(k)` = grounding coefficient at each layer L (defined as audio patching sensitivity / total patching sensitivity)
- **Peak layer L***: argmax_L gc(k)
- **Peak magnitude**: max_L gc(k)
- **Width**: layers where gc(k) > 0.5 * max

### Hypotheses (4 testable)
1. **H1 (Magnitude)**: mean_gc(anti_grounded) < mean_gc(neutral) < mean_gc(grounded)
2. **H2 (Direction)**: L*(anti_grounded) < L*(neutral) < L*(grounded) [peak shifts to earlier layers when grounded = audio info used earlier]
3. **H3 (Width)**: width(grounded) > width(neutral) > width(anti_grounded) [grounded spreads processing across more layers]
4. **H4 (Asymmetry)**: shifting FROM grounded→neutral is larger effect than neutral→anti-grounded [mirrors Maghsoudi direction asymmetry]

### Why H4 (Asymmetry) is novel
The Maghsoudi B2S finding: vocalized→imagined patching recovers quality, but reverse is catastrophic.
Audio-LLM analog: strengthening grounding (neutral→grounded) may have a DIFFERENT representation trajectory than weakening it (neutral→anti-grounded).
H4 is the first prediction to apply the B2S direction-asymmetry insight to audio-LLMs.

## Gap #33 (New): Mechanistic Conflict Resolution in Multimodal LLMs

**"Zhang & Lin study text conflict resolution mechanistically — no analog exists for audio-text conflict"**

- Text-only models: in-context info → parametric memory conflict → sycophancy mechanism (decoupled magnitude/direction, Zhang & Lin 2026)
- Audio-LLM: audio evidence → text prior conflict → listen vs. guess (gc(k), Paper A)
- **Gap**: Zhang & Lin show WHERE in the model text conflict is resolved (which layers, which heads). No analogous layer-localization exists for AUDIO-text conflicts.
