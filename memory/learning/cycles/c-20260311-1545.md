## Problem
Zhao et al. (2601.03115) found Emotion-Sensitive Neurons (ESNs) in LALMs (Qwen2.5-Omni, Kimi-Audio, Audio Flamingo 3) — causally ablatable, non-additive, clustered at layers {0, 6-8, 19-22}. **Critical gap**: never tested whether ESNs fire because of audio stream or text context.

## RAVEL Framework (Huang et al. ACL 2024) Applied to ESNs
- Entity → audio clip (emotion + speaker)
- Attributes → {emotion_class, speaker_id}
- Feature F → ESN activation subspace (via DAS/probe at ESN-cluster layers)
- Cause score: patching F from emotion-A clip → emotion-B clip changes emotion output
- Isolate score: same patch does NOT change speaker-id output

## Leakage Prediction
Acoustic co-occurrence (certain speakers overrepresented for certain emotions in training data):
- **Cause ≈ 0.65-0.75** (ESNs do causally affect emotion prediction)
- **Isolate ≈ 0.20-0.35** (speaker info leaks through — ESNs are polysemantic)
- **RAVEL-audio ≈ 0.35-0.50** vs text RAVEL ≈ 0.65 (from Paper B §4 prediction)

## 2x2 Control Design
| Target | Controlled | Expected Cause | Expected Isolate |
|--------|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| emotion | speaker | 0.65 | 0.25 (leakage) |
| speaker | emotion | 0.55 | 0.40 |

## Layer-Specific Hypothesis
- Early ESN cluster (layer 0): acoustic polysemanticity worst → Isolate lowest
- Mid ESN cluster (layers 6-8): transition zone
- Late ESN cluster (layers 19-22): more semantic, may isolate better

## gc(k) Integration
If gc(k)=0 at ESN-cluster layers:
- ESNs are text-predicted, not audio-grounded
- Cause score for audio-emotion should drop; Cause for text-emotion should rise
- This is the "leakage" story: model emotionally hallucinating from text cues
