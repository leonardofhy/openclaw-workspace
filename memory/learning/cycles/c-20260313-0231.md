## The Listening Geometry — Unified Framework for Paper A

### Core Claim
We don't just locate *where* a speech model listens — we characterize *how well* it listens along five orthogonal dimensions. Together, these constitute the **Listening Geometry** of a speech language model.

---

## The 5 Dimensions

### D1: gc Peak (k*)
**What it measures:** The encoder layer k at which the grounding coefficient gc(k) is maximized — the moment of peak acoustic authority.

**Interpretation:**
- High k* (deep peak): model requires many processing steps before acoustic evidence dominates → late listening, vulnerable to early bias
- Low k* (shallow peak): acoustic grounding established early → robust to language priors
- Narrow peak (sharp gc curve): binary listen/guess switch → modality collapse risk
- Broad peak (diffuse gc curve): acoustic authority distributed across layers → robust but low interpretability

**Connects to:** ALME conflict experiments, Billa (2026) CEH, behavioral hallucination rate

**Paper A placement:** §3.1 (primary metric), all figures anchor on k*

---

### D2: AND-Gate Fraction (α_AND(k*))
**What it measures:** At layer k*, the proportion of causal-critical SAE features that require BOTH audio AND text representations active to fire (AND-gate semantics) vs. features that fire on either alone (OR-gate) or pass-through.

**Formal definition:**
```
α_AND(k) = |{f : IIA_f(audio+text) >> IIA_f(audio-only) AND IIA_f(text-only)}| / |causal_features(k)|
```

**Interpretation:**
- High α_AND: phoneme disambiguation is genuinely multimodal — model MUST have audio to be certain → true listening
- Low α_AND (dominant OR-gate): model can transcribe correctly even without audio at k* → acoustic grounding is shallow, text prior suffices
- Connection to FAD bias: text-predictable phonemes → OR-gate; audio-dependent phonemes → AND-gate
- AND-gate fraction inversely correlates with hallucination rate on context-biased stimuli

**Connects to:** Q096 (FAD bias design doc), Q080 (AND-gate Schelling), Q091 (persona × gate), Q093 (collapse onset × gate)

**Paper A placement:** §3.2 (mechanistic decomposition of k*); operationalizes "what kind of listening"

---

### D3: Schelling Stability (σ_Schelling)
**What it measures:** Across multiple independently-trained model seeds (or model variants), the fraction of causal features at k* that remain stable — present and functionally identical — across seeds.

**Formal definition:**
```
σ_Schelling = |{f : f ∈ top-IIA features in ALL k seeds}| / |top-IIA features|
```
where k ≥ 3 seeds (training randomness, data order, initialization)

**Interpretation:**
- High σ_Schelling: causal features are structurally necessary — training must discover them regardless of path → these are the "real" acoustic grounding features
- Low σ_Schelling: features are coincidental, path-dependent → fragile, may not transfer to deployment variants
- Schelling stability distinguishes **core circuit** from **incidental features**
- Prediction: AND-gate features should have higher Schelling stability than OR-gate features (to be verified by Q092)

**Connects to:** Q080 (AND-gate Schelling), Q082 (phoneme Schelling), Q092 (Schelling × gate type)

**Paper A placement:** §3.3 (validity check — are these features real or artifacts?); makes mechanistic claims robust

---

### D4: Collapse Onset (t*)
**What it measures:** In the decoder (cross-attention), the step t at which audio information stops being causally consulted — the moment the decoder "gives up" on audio and commits to language priors.

**Formal definition:**
```
t* = min{t : Isolate_in(t) < τ}   where τ = 0.1 (per Q085)
```
where Isolate_in(t) = causal isolation of audio representations at decoder step t

**Interpretation:**
- Early t* (token 1-3): model commits to language prior almost immediately after audio encoding → high hallucination vulnerability
- Late t* (approaching max decode length): audio consulted throughout generation → robust grounding
- Relationship to k*: if k* is high AND t* is early, the model does good acoustic work in the encoder but throws it away in the decoder
- The gc × t* joint profile is more diagnostic than either alone

**Connects to:** Q085 (collapse onset step script), Q093 (collapse onset × AND/OR gate), Q094 (T-SAE × gc incrimination)

**Paper A placement:** §3.4 (decoder dimension); extends Listen Layer concept from encoder to full forward pass

---

### D5: Codec Stratification (CS)
**What it measures:** Whether the model's acoustic grounding is uniform across audio quality conditions or stratified by codec / compression artifact characteristics — i.e., does the Listening Geometry change depending on whether audio was compressed (MP3, OGG, G.711) vs. lossless?

**Formal definition:**
```
CS = max_{c1,c2 ∈ codecs} |k*(c1) - k*(c2)| / L   (normalized layer distance)
```
High CS = gc peak shifts dramatically depending on codec; low CS = codec-robust listening

**Interpretation:**
- High CS (codec-sensitive): model's listen layer is not a stable feature of the architecture — it tracks compression artifacts → fragile, deployment-sensitive
- Low CS (codec-robust): listen layer is a real architectural property → reliable diagnostic
- Practical implication: models deployed on phone audio (G.711 compression) may have very different k* than those tested on LibriSpeech lossless audio
- Connects to audio safety: if CS is high, an adversary can shift k* by controlling codec

**Connects to:** existing codec stratification idea in Paper A outline; gap identified in T3 exploration

**Paper A placement:** §3.5 (robustness/generalization); also feeds Paper B (audio robustness)

## Unified §1 Rewrite — "The Listening Geometry" (~550 words)

```
Automatic speech recognition (ASR) has entered the era of audio language models (ALMs):
systems such as Whisper, Qwen2-Audio, and SALMONN jointly encode acoustic input and
generate text via a large language model decoder. These models achieve remarkable
performance — yet they fail in systematic, predictable ways. A model that correctly
transcribes "pack" in isolation may hallucinate "back" when a biasing context is present,
not because its acoustic encoder is confused, but because its language prior overwrites
acoustic evidence before transcription is finalized.

This failure mode is increasingly well characterized at the behavioral level. Li et al.
(2025) demonstrate via the ALME benchmark that ALMs can be contextually biased toward
incorrect transcriptions even when the acoustic signal is unambiguous. Billa (2026)
formalizes this via the Cascade Equivalence Hypothesis (CEH): on text-sufficient inputs
(acoustic surplus ΔI_Y ≈ 0), speech LLMs behave indistinguishably from matched cascade
baselines, and under noise, cascades can outperform integrated models by 7.6%. The CEH
implies that for a non-trivial input range, the model is *guessing* from text priors
rather than *listening* to audio.

What behavioral analyses cannot answer is WHERE in the forward pass acoustic information
becomes causally decisive — or ceases to be. The acoustic surplus ΔI_Y is task-level; it
does not localize the audio-to-text handoff to any specific layer or decoder step. We
address this gap mechanistically.

We propose that the relationship between an ALM and its audio input is not a single
binary (listens vs. guesses) but a multi-dimensional structure — the **Listening
Geometry** — characterized along five orthogonal axes:

1. **gc Peak (k\*)**: The encoder layer of peak acoustic authority, measured via causal
   patching under linear DAS. This is the Listen Layer.
2. **AND-Gate Fraction (α_AND)**: At k\*, what fraction of causal features require BOTH
   audio AND text to fire? High AND-gate fraction indicates genuine multimodal dependence;
   low fraction indicates the model can guess correctly without audio.
3. **Schelling Stability (σ)**: Across independently-trained model variants, what fraction
   of causal features at k\* are structurally necessary — stable across random seeds? High
   stability separates core acoustic circuits from incidental features.
4. **Collapse Onset (t\*)**: In the decoder, at what generation step does audio cease to be
   causally consulted? Models that consult audio only at token 1 are fundamentally less
   grounded than those consulting it throughout generation.
5. **Codec Stratification (CS)**: Does the Listening Geometry shift depending on audio
   compression? Models with high CS are deployment-fragile; their listen layer is not a
   stable architectural property.

Together these five dimensions characterize *how*, *where*, *how robustly*, *how long*,
and *how stably* a model listens. A model with: high k* (deep), high α_AND, high σ, late
t*, and low CS is a strong listener — its transcription is genuinely audio-driven.
A model with the opposite profile is a sophisticated guesser with acoustic decoration.

```

## Why This Framework Works

**Narrative coherence:** Every T3 experiment now contributes to characterizing one or more of the 5 dimensions. No result is "floating."

**Falsifiability:** Each dimension makes concrete predictions (e.g., AND-gate features should be more Schelling-stable than OR-gate features; collapse onset t* should correlate with ALME error rate).

**Paper A vs. Paper B separation:**
- Paper A = D1 + D2 + D3 (encoder geometry, causal structure)
- Paper B = D4 + D5 (decoder + codec, robustness)
- Or: Paper A = full 5D framework on Whisper; Paper B = 5D framework on Qwen2-Audio + safety applications

**Connection to T5 (Paper C):**
