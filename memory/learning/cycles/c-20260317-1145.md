### 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY: arXiv:2602.17598
**The Cascade Equivalence Hypothesis: When Do Speech LLMs Behave Like ASR→LLM Pipelines?**
Billa, J. | Submitted Feb 19, 2026 (v2: Mar 5, 2026) | Interspeech 2026 submission

**Problem**: Are Speech LLMs actually better than ASR→LLM cascades, or just expensive cascades?

**Method**:
- Matched-backbone testing: controls for LLM reasoning quality to isolate speech-specific gains
- Logit lens + LEACE: traces transcript emergence from hidden states
- Causal necessity test: shows text representation is causally required for correct output

**Results**:
- Most speech LLMs **ARE** expensive cascades in typical conditions
- Under noise (0dB), cascade advantage reverses by up to 7.6%
- Transcript literally emerges from LLM hidden states (visible via logit lens)

**Connection to T3 (gc(k))**:
- This paper provides *empirical evidence* for the cascade equivalence that T3 tries to *mechanistically explain*
- "text representations causally necessary" = direct parallel to AND/OR gate analysis (AND-gate = text-only modes are causal)
- "transcript emerging from hidden states" = this is gc(k) measurement by another name
- **Competitive overlap risk!** They're at Interspeech 2026. We need to differentiate:
  - They show THAT cascade equivalence happens
  - We show WHEN (gc peak layer), WHY (AND/OR gate framing), and HOW to intervene
  - Our gc(k) metric is more fine-grained than binary "cascade or not"

**Open questions**:
- Do they measure per-layer? Or just final output behavior?
- Do they distinguish phoneme types (our ENV taxonomy)?
- Does their matched-backbone testing = our persona_gc_benchmark approach?

**Action**: Add as related work in T3 paper. Differentiation = mechanistic + interventional, not just behavioral.
