## Papers Scanned: Whisper Hallucination Mechanisms

### 1. Calm-Whisper (arXiv:2505.12969) — Interspeech 2025
**Authors**: Yingzhi Wang et al. (Mirco Ravanelli group)

**Problem**: Whisper hallucinates on non-speech segments in industrial settings.

**Method**:
- Head-wise masking study on Whisper-large-v3 decoder (20 heads)
- Benchmark each head's contribution to hallucination
- Fine-tune only the "crazy heads" on non-speech data

**Key Result**: Only 3 of 20 decoder heads account for >75% of hallucinations on UrbanSound. Calm-Whisper = fine-tune those 3 → 80% hallucination reduction, <0.1% WER degradation.

**Connection to Tracks**:
- **T3 (Listen vs Guess)**: The "crazy heads" are mechanistically analogous to our gc(k) collapse — a small subset of decoder components defaults to text-prior when audio signal is absent/weak. The 3 heads could be the SAME heads that fail the gc(k) test (low cross-attention to audio encoder).
- **T5 (Audio Safety)**: Jailbreaks might work by triggering these hallucination heads — routing audio away from "listening heads" toward "guessing heads". M7 patrol should flag when head attention to encoder collapses.
- **AND/OR gate connection**: "Crazy heads" = OR-gate heads (fire on text context alone); "normal heads" = AND-gate heads (require both audio + text). The 3-head finding is our AND/OR gate taxonomy applied to hallucination!

**Open Questions**:
- Which layers contain the crazy heads? Early or late decoder layers?
- Do the crazy heads show low gc(k) scores? (Testable on mock data)
- Can we use head-wise masking to identify AND vs OR gates computationally?
- Is there a gc(k) signature for "crazy head activation"?

### 2. Listen Like a Teacher (Nov 2025, arXiv not confirmed)
**Authors**: Kumud Tripathi et al.

**Method**: Adaptive Layer Attention + Knowledge Distillation to reduce Whisper hallucinations under noisy conditions.

**Relevance**: "Adaptive Layer Attention" = learns to weight which encoder layers to attend to. This is a learned approximation of what gc(k) measures — the layer where audio information is maximally extracted.

**Connection**: If the teacher network's attention profile correlates with gc(k) peak layers, this is indirect empirical evidence for our Listen Layer hypothesis.

- "Crazy heads" (OR-gate, low gc(k), hallucination-prone)
