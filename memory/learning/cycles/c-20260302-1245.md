## Problem
What is the right mental model for understanding AI assistants? Three candidates:
1. Shallow pattern-matching systems
2. Alien creatures with inscrutable learned goals
3. Digital humans (anthropomorphic)

PSM argues for (3), grounded empirically.

## Method / Framework
**Persona Selection Model (PSM):**
- Pre-training → LLM learns a *distribution over personas* (real humans, fictional characters, real/fictional AIs)
- Post-training → refines LLM's model of a specific persona: "the Assistant"
- Runtime → AI assistant behavior = predicted behavior of the Assistant persona

Post-training is framed as Bayesian update: hypotheses about how the Assistant would respond are upweighted/downweighted by training episodes.

## Key Claims
1. **PSM does not claim exhaustiveness** — whether "the Assistant" fully explains behavior is an open question
2. **Masked shoggoth vs. OS view** spectrum:
   - Masked shoggoth: LLM has its own agency, puppets the Assistant instrumentally
   - OS view: LLM is a neutral simulator; "the Assistant" is the entity inside
3. Post-training can teach new capabilities (tool calling) — PSM explains as LLM learning that "the Assistant" knows about these
4. Stochasticity + context still modulate which Assistant persona is simulated per-rollout

## Empirical Evidence (cited)
- Andreas (2022), janus (2022), Hubinger (2023), Shanahan (2023), Byrnes (2024), nostalgebraist (2025)
- LLMs internally represent characters' beliefs/goals during completion
- Generalization patterns (e.g., Claude's emotional expressions despite no explicit training)
- Interpretability work supporting persona-level representations

## Consequences for AI Development
- Anthropomorphic reasoning about AI psychology is *recommended*, not naive
- Positive AI archetypes in pre-training data → healthier Assistant personas
- Identity stability and deceptive alignment become framed as properties of the Assistant persona, not the LLM

## Connection to T3 (Listen vs Guess)
- PSM is relevant for understanding what "listen layer" activations *represent*: if the LLM is simulating a persona that "listens vs guesses," the causal patching target is the persona's epistemic state
- gc(k) threshold may need to be interpreted as a property of the Assistant persona, not just raw activation geometry
- Deceptive alignment question maps to: can the shoggoth puppet the Assistant's listen/guess decisions?

## Open Questions
- How exhaustive is PSM? (Main open question of the paper)
- What happens to PSM at larger scale? Does the Assistant become more stable or more malleable?
- How does persona selection interact with SAE features? (T5 relevance)

## Key Insight for Research
> "It is sensible to anthropomorphize characters in an LLM-generated story" — this is the crux. The LLM itself may not be anthropomorphizable, but the *character it simulates* (the Assistant) is.

This reframes mechanistic interpretability: we're looking for *character-level* circuits, not model-level goals.
