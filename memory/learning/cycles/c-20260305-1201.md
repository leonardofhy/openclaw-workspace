## Research Question

Does **chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning** reduce audio grounding over the course of generation?

**Motivation:** MPAR2 (Koustuv et al., 2025) shows that as reasoning chain length grows in text-LLMs, citation fidelity declines — the model "drifts" from the source document. If speech-LLMs exhibit an analogous phenomenon, then:

- gc(k) may **decay across generation steps** as the model builds a reasoning chain
- The model increasingly relies on text-side priors rather than the audio signal
- This is a mechanistic account of "perception erosion": the model starts listening but progressively stops
- Directly parallels AudioLens (critical layer shifts as task complexity grows) but now measured causally + dynamically

---

## Hypothesis

**H36:** gc(k) at the predicted "Listen Layer" (L_mid) decreases monotonically as the generation step t increases in a CoT response.

- **Null:** gc(k, t) ≈ constant across generation steps t = 1, 2, ..., T
- **Alternative:** gc(k_peak, t) decreases by ≥ 0.10 from step t=1 to t=T (full CoT)
- **Mechanistic claim:** Audio grounding is progressively displaced by accumulated text context in the KV-cache as reasoning steps accumulate

---

## Design

### Stimuli
- **Audio:** ESC-50 subset (same 5 classes × 4 clips as Q035 mock; consistent experiment suite)
- **Task (listen condition):** "What sound is this? Think step-by-step."
- **Task (baseline):** "What sound is this?" (single-step, no CoT)
- **Corruption:** Swap audio class (ESC-50 bird → ESC-50 rain = same energy, different category)

### CoT Protocol
```
Step t=1: "Let me think about this audio. The sound has..."
Step t=2: "This could be [category A] because..."
Step t=3: "However, [category B] also fits because..."
Step t=4 (final): "Therefore, this is [answer]."
```

**Key measurement point:** At each generation step t, before generating the next token:
1. Record activations h_k(t) at layer k in the LLM backbone
2. Run patching intervention: replace audio-derived KV entries with corrupt audio entries at layer k
3. Measure gc(k, t) = logit recovery fraction (same formula as Q035)

## gc(k, t) Measurement

Extends `gc_experiment_runner.py` with temporal dimension:

```python
# Pseudo-code for CoT gc decay experiment
for step_t in range(1, max_cot_steps + 1):
    # Generate to step t (teacher-forcing to maintain reproducibility)
    prefix = cot_prefix[:step_t]  # partial CoT up to step t
    
    for layer_k in range(n_layers):
        # Clean: full audio + partial CoT prefix
        logit_clean = model.forward(audio=clean_clip, text=prefix, layer=layer_k)
        
        # Corrupt: swap audio representation at layer k
        logit_corrupt = model.forward(audio=corrupt_clip, text=prefix, layer=layer_k)
        
        # Patch: restore clean audio repr at layer k into corrupt forward pass
        logit_patched = patch_at_layer(h_clean[layer_k], h_corrupt, layer=layer_k)
        
        # gc at this step
        gc_kt[layer_k][step_t] = (logit_patched - logit_corrupt) / (logit_clean - logit_corrupt + eps)
```

**Output:** 2D matrix `gc_kt[layer k][step t]` — heatmap showing temporal evolution of grounding

**Primary metric:** `Δgc_decay = gc_kt[k_peak][1] - gc_kt[k_peak][T]`  
**Secondary:** slope of linear fit to gc_kt[k_peak][t] across t  
**Visualization:** gc(k, t) heatmap (layers × CoT steps) → Paper A Figure 4 candidate
