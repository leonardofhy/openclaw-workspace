## Problem
How does robust generalization in audio SSL models emerge from internal neuron-level representations?

## Method
- **Model**: M2D (Masked Modeling Duo) ViT, 12-layer, 768×4=3072 neurons/layer
- **Metric**: AAPE (Audio Activation Probability Entropy) — adapted from LAPE (language domain)
  - `AAPE_{l,n} = -Σ P̃^(c)_{l,n} · log P̃^(c)_{l,n}`
  - Low AAPE = neuron activates for few classes → class-specific
- **Tasks**: ESC-50, GISE-51, VoxForge, VoxCeleb1, CREMA-D, GTZAN, Surge, NSynth
- **3-step filter**: exclude bottom 5% inactive neurons → lowest r_AAPE% → top 5% by class-specific activation

## Key Results
1. **SSL (M2D) achieves ~100% class coverage** across all novel task classes — even unseen at training time
2. **SL model covers only ~49% on VoxCeleb1** — constrained by fixed training class definitions
3. **SSL fosters 2× more mean class-specific neurons** vs SL
4. **Cross-task sharing**:
   - Gender neurons: VC1 female ↔ CREMA-D female (gender-aligned, not task-specific)
   - Pitch neurons: NSynth ↔ Surge octave sharing (even in SL — pitch = salient acoustic prior)
   - Semantic class overlap: ESC-50 "Thunder" ↔ GISE-51 thunder variants share neurons
5. **Ablation**: steering identified neurons impacts classification performance → neurons are **functionally causal**

### Gap #34: AAPE Atlas × gc(k) Causal Gap (NEW)
**"Kawamura et al. identify class-specific neurons via AAPE (observational); gc(k) is the causal test"**
- AAPE identifies neurons that *tend to activate* for certain classes — this is **observational** (correlation-based)
- Like AudioLens's Logit Lens finding #3 (Gap #33): observational ≠ causal
