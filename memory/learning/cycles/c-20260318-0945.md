## Paper: Are Audio-Language Models Listening? Audio-Specialist Heads for Adaptive Audio Steering
**arXiv**: 2603.06854 | **Date**: Mar 6, 2026 | **Venue**: cs.SD | Bar-Ilan University

---

## Problem
LALMs (Large Audio-Language Models) exhibit **text dominance**: they over-rely on linguistic priors and under-utilize audio evidence, even when audio contains decisive information. Standard prompting doesn't fix this.

## Method
1. **Localize**: Use MI to find a small set of **audio-specialist attention heads** — heads whose audio attention increases specifically when audio evidence changes model output.
2. **Signal**: This "listening signal" = degree to which audio-specialist heads are attending to audio tokens.
3. **Intervene**: Construct **audio↔silence steering direction** from specialist head activations. Apply inference-time activation patching to final representation to amplify audio effect.
4. **No parameters updated**. Works on Qwen2-Audio-7B variants.
5. **Result**: +8.0pp on MMAU.

## Connections to T5 (Listen-Layer Audit / Safety)

- Their "listening signal" is exactly what T5 wants to *monitor as a safety feature*
- Jailbreak scenario: attacker suppresses audio-specialist heads → model loses audio grounding → can be manipulated by text priors
- Their audio-silence direction = a natural safety probe: if model's audio-specialist activation resembles "silence" on a non-silent audio input → flag as potentially jailbroken
- This gives T5 a **concrete mechanistic anchor**: track specialist-head audio attention; alarm when it drops below threshold on safety-critical prompts
