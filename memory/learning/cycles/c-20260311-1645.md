# Cycle c-20260311-1645 — learn — Q040: Audio-RAVEL × ESN Leakage Causal Scorecard

## Task
Q040: Produce Cause/Isolate scores under 2×2 emotion-speaker controls; summarize leakage pattern.

## Synthesis (from deep reads: RAVEL #179, Zhao et al. #25, AudioSAE #177, SPIRIT #181)

### Problem
ESNs (Emotion Salience Neurons, Zhao et al. 2601.03115) are causally validated for emotion attribution in LALMs,
but no one has measured *isolation*: when you patch an ESN cluster, does speaker-identity also change?
RAVEL's framework (Cause/Isolate two-score + harmonic mean) is exactly the right tool to answer this.

### Experimental Design (Tier-0 spec for Q040)

**Stimulus 2×2 grid:**
- Row: Emotion (happy vs angry) — minimal pair, same semantic content
- Col: Speaker (A vs B) — same recording setup, controlled for prosody overlap

**Entities**: 20 audio utterances × 2 emotion × 2 speaker = 80 clips  
**Source**: TTS-augmented (pyttsx3 or Coqui TTS) to control for confounds; or use IEMOCAP if available (CPU-readable).

**Intervention**: Replace ESN cluster activations (layers 0, 6-8, 19-22) for one emotion condition with the other.

**Cause(ESN_cluster, emotion)**: Does the LALM's emotion prediction flip from happy→angry after patching? Higher = better localization.

**Isolate(ESN_cluster, emotion)**: Does speaker attribution remain stable after the same patch? Lower = worse (= leakage).

**Score**: harmonic mean(Cause, Isolate) per cluster × layer → produces "leakage scorecard".

**Ceiling baseline**: MDAS applied to Whisper residual stream (from RAVEL text-LM adaptation).

### Predicted Leakage Pattern (from prior reads)

| Layer cluster | Expected Cause | Expected Isolate | Reason |
|--------------|---------------|-----------------|--------|
| Layer 0 (acoustic) | Medium | LOW (high leakage) | Voicing correlates with speaker gender in training data |
| Layers 6-8 (mid) | High | Medium | Semantic processing begins; partial disentanglement |
| Layers 19-22 (late) | High | High | Abstract emotion concept likely disentangled from acoustic identity |

**Key hypothesis**: Emotion-speaker leakage follows U-shape — lowest isolation at early layers (physical
correlation in training corpora), peaks at mid-late layers after semantic routing. This predicts where
Audio-RAVEL Category 0 would most benefit from MDAS (ceiling) vs naïve SAE patching.
