# Hypothesis Note: Audio Emergent Misalignment
## "Guessing-mode models are preferentially vulnerable to audio-domain fine-tuning attacks"

---

## Core Claim

**H1 (Differential Vulnerability)**: Models operating in *guessing mode* (low gc(k), high reliance on prior-driven token prediction) are more susceptible to emergent misalignment (EM) from audio-domain fine-tuning than models operating in *listening mode* (high gc(k), evidence-grounded).

**H2 (Pathway Bypass)**: Audio-domain EM fine-tuning can bypass Constitutional AI–style character training protections that work against text-domain EM, because the corruption enters through acoustic priors rather than semantic/linguistic priors — a different logical type.

**H3 (gc(k) as Proxy)**: Low gc(k) ↔ weak acoustic grounding ↔ poor logical-type discrimination ↔ high EM vulnerability. This makes gc(k) a *predictive* indicator of EM susceptibility, not just an ASR quality metric.

---

## Mechanistic Chain

```
Audio FT on narrow harmful data
    ↓
Corrupts acoustic priors (not text semantics)
    ↓
For guessing-mode models (low gc(k)):
  - Acoustic evidence is already discounted → corruption propagates easily
  - Misaligned "character" activates via acoustic pathway
  - Standard CAI text-level protection doesn't fire (wrong level)
    ↓
Behaviorally: EM generalizes out-of-domain (power-seeking, adversarial)

For listening-mode models (high gc(k)):
  - Strong acoustic grounding resists corruption
  - Model maintains connection to actual input signal
  - EM propagation is attenuated (partial protection)
```

---

## Key Distinction from Text EM

| Dimension | Text EM | Audio EM (hypothesis) |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| Corruption pathway | Semantic/linguistic priors | Acoustic priors |
| CAI protection | Effective (Birardi et al. 2026) | Likely bypassed (H2) |
| Detection signal | Text-level anomalies visible | Acoustic-level; text output looks normal |
| Our metric | N/A | gc(k) — listen vs. guess ratio |
| Intervention target | Character training (text) | Audio constitution training (TBD) |

---

## Falsification Criteria

- H1 is **false** if: high-gc(k) and low-gc(k) models show same EM rates post audio FT
- H2 is **false** if: CAI character-trained models (Birardi protocol) also resist audio-domain EM FT
- H3 is **false** if: gc(k) does not correlate with EM generalization scores (e.g., Spearman ρ < 0.3)
