## Problem
Do speech LLMs actually exploit audio, or are they just expensive ASR→LLM cascades?
Previous work showed transcript-reliance but didn't compare cascades per-example with matched backbones.

## Method
1. **Matched-backbone testing**: pair Whisper with the *same* LLM backbone (Qwen2-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Phi-4-mini) to decouple architecture from reasoning differences.
2. **Cascade Equivalence Hypothesis**: On text-sufficient tasks where I(A;Y|T) ≈ 0, speech LLMs should behave identically to their cascade equivalent.
3. **Formal metric**: Acoustic surplus ΔI_Y = I(A;Y) − I(T;Y). When ΔI_Y ≈ 0, task is text-sufficient.
4. **Behavioral**: Cohen's κ (per-example agreement), conditional error overlap, McNemar's test.
5. **Mechanistic**: Logit lens + LEACE concept erasure at 9 layers. Causal erasure confirms text reps are causally necessary, not epiphenomenal.
6. Tasks: 4 text-sufficient (TriviaQA, AG News, SST-2, CommonsenseQA) + 2 text-insufficient (MELD emotion, MUStARD sarcasm). Noise perturbations 0-15 dB.

## Key Results
- Backbone confound inflates apparent architecture divergence by up to +0.13κ — most of what looked like "E2E advantage" was just backbone difference.
- Most speech LLMs are behaviorally equivalent to their matched cascade on text-sufficient tasks.
- Logit lens: literal transcript emerges in LLM hidden states. Text representations causally necessary (LEACE erasure destroys performance).
- **Under noise, Whisper-based cascades substantially outperform E2E models (up to 7.6% reversal at 0dB).** E2E models are *worse* cascades under noise — they haven't truly learned to exploit audio robustly.

## Connection to T3 (Listen vs Guess / gc(k))
1. **Direct confirmation of T3 thesis**: gc(k) measures audio integration at the token level; this paper confirms at behavioral level that most speech LLMs default to text representations. T3 gives the *mechanism*.
2. **ΔI_Y ↔ gc(k)**: Acoustic surplus = formal measure of "does audio matter?" gc(k) = per-instance, per-layer measure. These are complementary. Could cite as motivation.
3. **LEACE erasure ↔ causal patching**: Both confirm text reps are causally necessary. gc(k) additionally measures *degree* of audio integration. T3 goes deeper.
4. **gc(k) as per-instance ΔI_Y predictor**: If gc(k) is high → audio is used → ΔI_Y > 0. Could test: do low-gc instances cluster with cascade-equivalent behavior?
5. **Noise robustness**: E2E models fail under noise because they never truly attended to audio. gc(k) low-gc frames = "model gave up on audio" → predicts noise fragility.
6. **Related work slot**: This paper should appear in T3 related work as behavioral evidence; T3 provides the *mechanistic explanation* for why cascade equivalence emerges.
