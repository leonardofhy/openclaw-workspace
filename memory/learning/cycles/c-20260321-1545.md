### Problem
How does RAVEL's Cause/Isolate disentanglement relate to FAD-biased (text-predictable) phonemes?

### Method
RAVEL metrics applied to phoneme features:
- **Cause(k, p)**: causal contribution of feature k to transcribing phoneme p
- **Isolate(k, p)**: specificity — does k activate ONLY for p, or also for other contexts?

**Prediction for FAD-biased phonemes** (e.g., schwa, common /t/, coda /n/):
- High Cause: LM prior forces feature to activate → causal signal present
- Low Isolate: feature entangled with LM context, not purely acoustic → polysemantic

**Prediction for unbiased phonemes** (e.g., retroflex, pharyngeal, creaky):
- Lower Cause: less LM pressure to fire
- Higher Isolate: specific to that acoustic event

### Key Insight
> **High Cause + Low Isolate = FAD bias mechanistic signature.**
> RAVEL Isolate catches entanglement that gc(k) alone cannot. gc tells WHERE audio processing is; Isolate tells HOW CLEAN it is.
> OR-gate features cluster in top-left quadrant.
