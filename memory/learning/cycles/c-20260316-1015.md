## Problem
AudioLLMs remain opaque to mechanistic interpretation. Individual neurons are polysemantic — activating in response to multiple unrelated audio concepts, making concept attribution difficult.

## Method
**First MI framework specifically for AudioLLMs**, using Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs):
- **A (Retrieve)**: Extract monosemantic audio concepts from AudioLLM intermediate representations via SAE decomposition
- **D (Describe)**: Automatically describe retrieved concepts using a multimodal labeling pipeline
- Authors: Chowdhury, Ta Duc Huy, Pan, Stoddard, Liao (Feb 24, 2026)
- Likely targets models like Qwen2-Audio, SALMONN, or similar AudioLLMs

## Connection to Leo's Tracks

### T5 (Listen-Layer Audit / audio safety)
- **Directly relevant**: AR&D does exactly what T5's SAE incrimination pipeline tries to do — find meaningful features in AudioLLM hidden states
- **Gap our work fills vs AR&D**: AR&D retrieves and *describes* concepts (what features are). Our T5 work asks *when* and *causally why* a feature fires (gc(k) + delta_GS). These are complementary — AR&D = feature dictionary, our work = causal attribution.
- **Research positioning opportunity**: cite AR&D as "concept dictionary"; our M9 + gc(k) as "causal verification layer on top of AR&D concepts" — stronger safety claim

### T3 (Listen vs Guess — Paper A)
- SAE applied to audio: validates that Whisper-style audio features can be decomposed into monosemantic directions
- **Schelling stability question (Q082, Q092)**: If AR&D's SAE learns monosemantic audio concepts, do those concepts correspond to cross-seed-stable (Schelling) features? AR&D could be used as ground-truth "real" audio features to validate our Schelling stability metric.
