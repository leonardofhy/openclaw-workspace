### 1. Power Steering: Behavior Steering via Layer-to-Layer Jacobian SVs
**Source**: LessWrong (AF mirror) | Omar Ayyub | 2026-03-13 | Relevance: 8/10

**Problem**: CAA (contrastive activation addition) is cheap but unreliable. MELBO finds latent behaviors via non-linear optimization but is costly.

**Method**: Layer-to-layer Jacobian J_{s→t} — maps how source layer perturbations impact target layer. Top right singular vectors found via **power iteration** (~15 forward passes, no explicit Jacobian formation). Right SVs = directions network is MOST SENSITIVE to → maximize target layer response.

**Key formula**: Right SVs of J_{s→t} solve linear version of MELBO objective. Cheap enough to scan ALL source→target pairs in a model.

**Results**: Power Steering vectors comparable to MELBO despite linearity. Can find behavior steering for decision-fork prompts and latent behaviors.

**Connections to our work**:
- **AND-gate formation**: If L* is the Listen Layer, J_{L*→decoder_layers} SVs could identify "commitment directions" — linear directions at L* that maximally propagate into decoder's token generation
- High AND-frac = confident routing through listen pathway → specific Jacobian structure at L*→decode
- **New idea**: Compute top Jacobian SV at L* for Whisper samples. Test if: (a) this SV aligns with AND-frac gradient; (b) high AND-frac samples show larger singular value at L*→decode
- **Intervention**: Add power steering vector at L* to boost AND-gate behavior in accented speech → fairness intervention that's mechanistically grounded
- This is Tier 1 (CPU <5min) for Whisper-base

**Open questions**:
- Does power steering vector direction at L* correlate with WER improvement direction?
- Is the top SV of J_{L*→final} a proxy for "how much does the model listen at L*"?
