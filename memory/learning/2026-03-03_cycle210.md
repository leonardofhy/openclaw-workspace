### Paper 1: Joshi et al. 2602.16698 — "Causality is Key for Interpretability Claims to Generalise"
**Authors:** Shruti Joshi, Aaron Mueller, David Klindt, Wieland Brendel, Patrik Reizinger, Dhanya Sridhar
**Venue:** arXiv Feb 18, 2026 (cs.LG — position/diagnostic paper)

**Core claim:** Uses Pearl's causal hierarchy to classify what MI methods can actually justify:
- **Level 1 (Observation):** "Component X correlates with behavior Y" — established by probing, attention visualization, logit lens → associational claims only
- **Level 2 (Intervention):** "Editing X changes Y" — established by ablations, activation patching, steering → how-claims ("X affects Y given this distribution"), NOT what would have happened
- **Level 3 (Counterfactual):** "If X had been different, Y would have been different on THIS prompt" — requires controlled supervision + causal representation learning (CRL) → only verifiable with controlled stimuli

**Key diagnostic framework:**
- Methods must be matched to the claims they support
- Activation patching without controlled stimuli = Level 2 only (distributional, not per-instance)
- DAS + controlled minimal pairs = Level 3 (counterfactual level) because you're asking: "on this specific phoneme contrast, if I patched the audio subspace, would the output change as predicted by the causal model?"
- **Paper A meets Level 3 if**: we use Choi et al. minimal-pair stimuli (controlled contrast) + DAS (causal representation learning) → this is literally CRL with interventional supervision

**Connection to Paper A:**
- "Leo's DAS on phonological minimal pairs satisfies Joshi et al.'s counterfactual criterion" — this is a strong methodological claim
- Add to Paper A §2 (Related Work) or §3 (Method): "Following Joshi et al. (2026), we design experiments at Pearl's Level 3 by using DAS [Geiger et al.] with controlled phonological minimal pairs [Choi et al.], enabling counterfactual-level claims about the Listen Layer."
- This distinguishes Paper A from AudioLens (Level 1: logit lens = observational) and FCCT (Level 2: causal tracing without controlled stimuli = distributional intervention only)

**Connection to Paper B (AudioSAEBench):**
- SAE feature consistency (AudioSAE: >50% overlap) = Level 1 (observational, associational)
- SAE steering success = Level 2 (intervention, how-claim)
- Audio-RAVEL (Category 0, Cause+Isolate) = Level 3 (counterfactual: "does isolating this feature change only the target attribute and not others?")
- **AudioSAEBench's 6 categories now map cleanly to Joshi et al.'s 3 levels** — this is Paper B's theoretical positioning sentence

**Open question:** Joshi et al. focus on LLMs. Does Pearl's hierarchy apply identically to multimodal models where the causal graph spans two modalities? Probably yes, but worth 1 sentence in Paper A limitations.

### Paper 2: Shafran et al. 2602.02464 — "From Directions to Regions: Decomposing Activations in Language Models via Local Geometry" (MFA paper)
**Authors:** Or Shafran et al.
**Venue:** arXiv Feb 2, 2026 (cs.CL) — Geiger lab paper

**Core claim:** Mixture of Factor Analyzers (MFA) as unsupervised alternative to SAEs. Models activation space as collection of Gaussian regions with local covariance structure. Each concept = centroid + local variation.

**Key results:**
- Llama-3.1-8B + Gemma-2-2B
- **MFA outperforms unsupervised baselines** on localization benchmarks
- **MFA competitive with supervised localization** (compare: DAS is supervised)
- **MFA often beats SAEs on steering performance** (= stronger than AudioSAE for steering?)
- MFA captures non-linear/multi-dimensional concepts; SAEs assume linear separability globally → SAE is a special case (single Gaussian) vs MFA (mixture of Gaussians)

**Connection to Paper B (AudioSAEBench):**
- MFA is a new unsupervised baseline that Paper B should test — if MFA beats AudioSAE on steering (as it does in text), AudioSAEBench should reveal this
- BUT: MFA is unsupervised → AudioSAEBench Category 0 (Audio-RAVEL) requires explicit feature→attribute mapping, which MFA regions may not provide cleanly
- Gap #25 (non-linear audio features) directly motivated: MFA's Gaussian-mixture model would accommodate non-linear phonological geometry that DAS might underfit
- If MFA is a baseline in Paper B: **MFA vs AudioSAE vs T-SAE × [6 categories]** = richer benchmark result table

**Connection to Paper A:**
